To commemorate an industry


Postponed!

Terra Vista has decided it was about time the content authors, people developing the VRML spec, and the companies designing the tools, recieved the recognition they deserve. As a result the original release was issued on the Terra Vista mailing list back on August 9th, 1996.

Already Nominated!!!

the makers of the Suite have commited to a very genrous offer for a total of US$10K value of gifts to the award winners. The award ceremony will be taking place on-line and IDS will be providing the browser to enable the event for multi-user avatar and voice. A link will be provided to the download as the event approaches.

At present, both Howard Rheingold and Jaron Lanier have offered tentative agreements to participate in the event as guest speakers. As well, Robert Saint John has accepted to be a guest at the event as a speaker.

To the winner of an award will go the recognition duly deserved for a job well done, as well as a commemerative award in the form of a custom designed graphic and VRML object. At this time I would like to thank IDS for their generous offer as well as their sponsorship of the event.

If there is enough interest, the event will be spread across three days starting on December 6th and running to December 8th. Day one (Friday) will consist of an open house in the nominee showcase. Day two will be the Awards Ceremony itself. Finally, the third day will be for mini-seminars which will be conducted in multi-user enabled VRML "rooms". The seminars would involve speeches and debates on the technology of VR and VRML and the history and future of our great industry.

So, I would invite all of you to sit down, buckle your virtual seat belts, and be prepared to witness and participate in the future! It all points to a very exciting time as we approach a culmination and integration of _many_ hard years of work by leaders in the industry. May we all wake up to a brand new, brave world. Thank you for your interest and participation. You may get your browser at the door. Please park your horse in the back, by the trough. Len, mind getting your spaceship off the roof... =)
