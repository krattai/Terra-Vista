We interrupt the flow of the narrative for this brief announcement.

Water-based transportation is probably Earth's oldest profession, starting with the prokaryotes. Regarding human civilization, water-based transportation is arguably the most important system ever devised, since it carried people, goods, ideas, plants, animals and viruses all over the planet. But nothing stays on top forever. By the year 2002, river and oceanic traffic fell off in terms of relative industrial and commercial importance. The one significant exception to that remains the transportation of huge quantities of oil. Still, the days of the supertankers are numbered by the relentless march of technological substitution.
